Climate Change

What it is and its predominant causes.

Definition of Climate Change

When the climate patterns of either a region or the world change (and do not return to their previous state over time), then climate change is occurring. Until the Industrial Revolution 180 years ago, this was caused by natural cycles of heating and cooling, which develop over 10s of thousands of years. However, greenhouse gas emissions have altered this, resulting in a much greater heating effect. The consequences of this could be devastating, wreaking havoc on ecosystems and increasing sea levels, making parts of the Earth uninhabitable.

Causes of climate change:

  • Deforestation
  • Burning coal, gas, oil
  • Increasing livestock farming
  • Nitrogenous fertilisers
  • Fluorinated gases
